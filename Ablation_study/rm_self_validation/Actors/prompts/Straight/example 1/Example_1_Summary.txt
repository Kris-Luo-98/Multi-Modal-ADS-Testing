Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2005 Ford Focus, 4-door sedan was traveling north in the northbound lane of a two-lane, two-way road. Vehicle two (V2), a 1983 Buick Riviera, 2-door coupe was traveling south in the southbound lane of the same road. It was daylight, the weather was clear and the bituminous road surface was dry. The driver of V1 experienced a loss of control due to a pre-exisitng medical condition and V1 crossed the centerline andinto the path of V2. The driver of V2 was unable to avoid V1 and the front of V1 struck the front of V2 in an offset-frontal configuration. After impact V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in a private lawn just off the roadway. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the northbound lane facing south-southwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling vehicle damage. The 67-year-old female driver of V1 (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. She was transported via helicopter to a regional level-one trauma center and admitted with severe injuries.
